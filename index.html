💡 Why Hydration Matters
Water makes up about 60% of your body weight and is involved in every metabolic process. Even mild dehydration (1–2%) impairs cognitive function, reduces physical performance, and affects mood regulation.

The kidneys require adequate fluid to flush toxins, joints need water for lubrication, and your skin uses it for thermoregulation.
🧪 Signs of Dehydration
Watch for: dark yellow urine, fatigue, dizziness, dry mouth, headaches, and reduced cognitive focus. Your urine should be pale straw to light yellow when adequately hydrated.

🌊 Water Quality
Not all water is equal. Spring and mineral water naturally contain dissolved minerals. Tap water quality varies by region. Filtered water removes contaminants but may also remove beneficial minerals. Consider testing your water source.
